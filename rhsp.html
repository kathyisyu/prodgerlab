Rakai Health Sciences Program

After the first identification of clinical AIDS in Uganda, the Rakai Health Sciences Program was established as a collaboration between researchers at Makerere, Columbia and John Hopkins Universities, the Division of Intramural Research at the National Institutes of Allergy & Infectious diseases and the International Centre for Excellence Research (ICER). For over 30 years, RHSP has conducted clinical (HIV and CVD, Early Virological Monitoring) and immunological studies (HIV Phylogenetics, HIV Latent Viral Reservoir) to better understand the epidemiology, transmission, prevention and co-occurrence of communicable and non-communicable diseases in Uganda.Many of our studies are in collaboration with RHSP as our work is aimed at reducing the burden of HIV in sub-Saharan Africa.
