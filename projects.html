3D Foreskin Model

Our team is creating a 3D in vitro foreskin model through tissue engineering to model bacteria-human interactions. This artificial foreskin model can enable us to culture whole polymicrobial communities and test which anaerobes or combinations of anaerobes might be leading to the inflammatory immune responses that contribute to HIV susceptibility.

Foreskin Anaerobes

In collaboration with the Rakai Health Science Program, we are using foreskin tissues from Ugandan men who have been identified to have a high or low burden of anaerobes to determine if the men with anaerobes are more susceptible to infection, when exposed to relevant strains of HIV.

Latent Viral Reservoir

The persistence of the latent viral reservoir of HIV is the greatest barrier in achieving HIV cure. From clinical and immunological data provided by the Rakai Health Sciences Program, we are identifying predictors of reservoir size and potential targets for HIV cure modalities.

Penile Microbiota and Antimicrobial Agents

Through a randomized circumcision clinical trial, we are using immunofluorescence to assess epithelial integrity and HIV target cell density in foreskin tissue. We are also examining the effect of oral and topical antibiotic treatments on HIV susceptibility.
