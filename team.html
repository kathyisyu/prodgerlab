Our Team

Dr. Jessica Prodger

Dr. Prodger is currently a Canada Research Chair and assistant professor at Western University in the Department of Microbiology and Immunology and the Department of Epidemiology and Biostatistics. The goal of her work is to design new ways to prevent HIV transmission, which could have a significant impact on the HIV epidemic in sub-Saharan Africa. Her research is focused on understanding the interplay and communication between the penile microbiome and the human tissues, and how the relationship between bacteria and human cells can influence HIV.

David Zuanazzi

David obtained his PhD in Biochemistry from Western University in 2019. His work focused on applying mass spectrometry-based proteomics to diagnose infectious diseases in saliva, such as the Zika virus. David also holds a DDS degree and an MSc in periodontology from the Federal University of Rio de Janeiro, where he studied the colonization of the oral cavity by non-indigenous species that cause nosocomial infections. Spencer Yeung

Spencer earched his BMSc Honours Specialization in Physiology, MMASc Global Health Systems, and MSc Microbiology and Immunology at Western University. Spencer led the coordination of our lab's first clinical research study with Urologists in Toronto to collect foreskin tissues from adults undergoing male circumcision. These tissues were then used to optimize the ex vivo explant tissue model which can be used to assess the susceptibility of tissues to HIV. Our lab's vision is that this measured susceptibility can then be correlated with how a person's microbiome affects recruitment of immune cells to the foreskin tissue. In his work, to optimize the explant model, he competed viruses of different subtypes and measured their relative transmission efficiencies in both cervical and foreskin tissue.
